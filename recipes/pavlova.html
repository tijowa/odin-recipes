Pavlova

Description

Pavlova: invented in New Zealand, perfected in Australia. The dish has murky origins - meringue-and-fruit desserts have been around in Europe for a long time - but the popularity of the pavlova in Australasia is undeniable. It is distinguished from meringues by it's marshmallowy interior, and achieving this makes pavlova harder to perfect.

Ingredients

  • 6 egg whites
  • 170 g caster sugar
  • 1 tsp vinegar, ideally malt vinegar or apple cider vinegar
  • 2 tsp cornflour, sifted
  • ½ tsp vanilla

Equipment

A stand mixer is recommended for this recipe.

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 140 degrees Celsius
  2. Beat egg whites until stiff peaks form, then add the sugar and continue beating until glossy.
  3. Fold in the remaining ingredients.
  4. Using a spatula, transfer the mixture to a prepared tray, forming a circle 20 cm in diameter.
  5. Place in the oven on the bottom shelf, and bake for 2 hours.
  6. Switch off the oven, and allow to cool completely before removing.
  7. If it happens, even a cracked pavlova is delicious.
  8. To serve: top with whipped cream and lemon curd, and fresh fruit - e.g. passionfruit, mango, blueberries, and sliced strawberries.
