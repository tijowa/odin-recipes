Thai-style chicken salad

Description

This Thai-style salad is a satisfying as a standalone meal, or part of a larger Thai spread to share.

Ingredients

  • Tbsp oil (sesame or peanut would be great)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp chilli powder
  • 2 chicken thighs, skin on
  • 1/2 each of red, green, and yellow capsicum, julienned
  • 1 cup snow peas (mange tout)
  • 1 Lebanese cucumber, julienned
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 4 cups raw cabbage (or lettuce), shredded
  • 1 small onion, very finely sliced
  • 1/4 cup coriander
  • 1/4 cup mint
  • 1/4 cup Thai basil
  • 1 handful peanuts
  • chilli flakes to preferred taste (and heat)
  • 1 batch ginger-peanut dressing

Optional

  • crunchy fried shallots

Method

  1. Firstly, rub salt into the chicken, and follow this with the ground ginger and chilli powder. Allow to rest a few minutes - enough time to heat the oil over a medium heat in a frying pan or skillet.
  2. Brown whole chicken thighs on both sides, until the skin is crispy. When cooking longer would cause the skin to burn, remove chicken from the pan. Allow a moment to cool so it can be handled (or use a glove), then cut the chicken into medium dice, setting bones aside (for stock perhaps), and return meat to the pan to finish cooking all the way through. Remove from heat, set aside to cool.
  3. Toss vegetable components together with 3/4 of the dressing. Place the chicken on top, and pour across the remained of the dressing. Add peanuts, chilli flakes to taste, and shallots if using these. Serve while the chicken is still warm.
